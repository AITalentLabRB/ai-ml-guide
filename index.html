Common Confusion Points

Who Does What

ConfusionThe Actual DifferenceHow to Distinguish Quickly
Research Scientist vs. Applied ML ScientistRS invents new ML methods and publishes. Applied ML Scientist applies existing methods to product problems.Ask: "Tell me about a paper you've published." RS has an answer. Applied ML Scientist often doesn't — and that's fine.
Applied ML Scientist vs. ML EngineerApplied ML Scientist owns the modeling problem. ML Engineer owns the engineering problem.Check portfolio: does work center on model artifacts, or distributed systems and infra?
ML Engineer vs. Applied AI / GenAI EngineerML Engineer builds/optimizes models. GenAI Engineer integrates existing LLMs into products.Ask: "When did you last write a training loop?" ML Engineers do it regularly. GenAI Engineers rarely train from scratch.
MLOps vs. LLMOpsMLOps is general ML infra. LLMOps is LLM-specific (evals, prompt versioning, fine-tune orchestration).Ask about their stack: Kubeflow/Metaflow vs. LangSmith/Weights & Biases/PromptLayer.
Data Scientist vs. Applied ML ScientistData Scientist derives insights. Applied ML Scientist builds models that run in production at scale.Ask: "What's the heaviest production model you own end to end?" DS often doesn't own prod models.
R1

ML Research Scientist

Invents novel algorithms, publishes findings, advances the field's theoretical foundations

Research Track
What This Role Actually Does

Formulates new research problems, designs experiments, implements and evaluates novel methods, and disseminates findings via papers and talks. Output is ideas and proofs-of-concept — not production systems.

Interview Format

Research presentation, ML theory deep-dives, paper discussion. Rarely includes Leetcode. The bar is essentially a peer academic review.

Skills
Deep Learning theoryPyTorch / JAXStrong math (probability, optimization, linear algebra)Published research (top venues)CUDA / GPU programmingTransformers / attention mechanismsProduction SWE NOT requiredSQL NOT required
Green Signals
  • First/second author at NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, CVPR, EMNLP
  • PhD from top ML program (MIT, Stanford, CMU, Berkeley, Toronto, Oxford)
  • Postdoc or research internship at a top lab
  • Growing citation count — work being built upon
Red Signals
  • No publications or only workshop papers for a senior role
  • Can't explain the intuition behind their own research
  • PhD on a non-ML topic pivoting without postdoc bridge
  • Claims research experience but portfolio is all applied product work
Sourcing Channels
Highest
Paper author lists at NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR/ACL proceedings (semanticscholar.org, paperswithcode.com, arxiv.org)
Highest
PhD program alumni lists — ML labs at Berkeley, Stanford, CMU, MIT, UofT, Oxford
Highest
Conference networking — NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR poster sessions
Medium
LinkedIn filtered by PhD + "Research Scientist" at lab names (DeepMind, FAIR, Google Brain, MSR, OpenAI)
R2

Research Engineer (RSE)

Implements research at scale; bridges algorithmic ideas and working, reproducible systems

Research Track
What This Role Actually Does

Builds infrastructure to test research ideas at scale: distributed training frameworks, experiment pipelines, custom CUDA kernels, evaluation harnesses. Does not need to generate novel ideas, but must have enough ML depth to implement complex papers correctly.

How It Differs From ML Engineer

Research Engineers sit inside research orgs and serve research velocity. ML Engineers sit inside product orgs and ship to users. Research Engineers tolerate higher ambiguity; ML Engineers optimize for reliability.

Skills
Strong software engineeringPyTorch / JAX internalsDistributed training (FSDP, DeepSpeed, Megatron)CUDA / Triton kernel writingReading and implementing ML papersC++ systems experiencePublications NOT required
Green Signals
  • GitHub with complex ML systems code — training loops, custom ops
  • Experience implementing papers (reference specific papers + repos)
  • Has worked inside a research lab or research-adjacent team
  • Strong SWE fundamentals plus ML domain knowledge
Red Signals
  • Pure SWE background with no ML implementation experience
  • ML experience is only at the Keras/high-level API layer
  • No evidence of working with large-scale training jobs
Sourcing Channels
Highest
GitHub — contributors to Hugging Face Transformers, Megatron-LM, DeepSpeed, trlX, LLaMA implementations
Highest
Current Research Engineers at OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere, DeepMind on LinkedIn
Medium
ML systems Discord servers (EleutherAI, Alignment Forum tech discussions)
R3

AI Safety / Alignment Researcher

Ensures AI systems behave as intended; works on interpretability, RLHF, robustness, and alignment

Research Track
What This Role Actually Does

Studies failure modes of large ML systems and develops techniques to prevent them. Sub-areas: interpretability, RLHF and Constitutional AI, red-teaming, robustness, and formal specification. Increasingly applied at every major lab as AI regulation pressures grow.

Skills
ML fundamentals (LLMs, transformers)RLHF / reward modelingMechanistic interpretabilityRed-teaming / adversarial evaluationFormal reasoning or decision theoryPhilosophy or ethics background
Green Signals
  • Published at AI safety venues or Alignment Forum technical posts
  • Prior work at Anthropic, ARC, MIRI, Redwood, DeepMind Safety
  • Interpretability work (probing, activation patching, circuit analysis)
Red Signals
  • Only policy/ethics background with no ML implementation ability
  • Conflates AI safety with cybersecurity or data privacy
  • No hands-on experience with LLMs or training processes
Sourcing Channels
Highest
Alignment Forum and LessWrong technical sequences
Highest
ARC Evals, Redwood Research, CHAI (UC Berkeley) alumni
Medium
MATS / ARENA / AI safety bootcamp graduates
S1

Applied ML Scientist

Applies ML methods to product and business problems; owns models from experimentation through production

Applied Science Track
What This Role Actually Does

Runs the full ML lifecycle: frames the problem, selects and fine-tunes models, runs A/B experiments, owns the production model, and iterates on business metrics. More product-oriented than a Research Scientist; more modeling-oriented than a Data Scientist.

Skills
Python + ML frameworks (PyTorch/TF/sklearn)Feature engineering & model selectionA/B testing methodologySQLFine-tuning / transfer learningModel evaluation & monitoringCausal inferencePublishing papers NOT expected
Interview Format

ML case study, coding (ML-focused), model debugging scenario, metrics discussion. Some companies add statistics/probability screen.

Green Signals
  • MS or PhD with production model ownership
  • Can speak to business impact of their models (lift, revenue, error rate)
  • Has run A/B tests and interpreted results with statistical rigor
Red Signals
  • Only research/academic experience with no production exposure
  • Résumé lists model names without outcomes
  • No evidence of cross-functional work with product/engineering
Sourcing Channels
Highest
LinkedIn "Applied Scientist" at Airbnb, Uber, Netflix, DoorDash, LinkedIn, Stripe
Highest
Kaggle Masters/Grandmasters
Medium
MS graduates from CMU MCDS, Stanford CS, UW with industry practicum

E1

Applied AI / GenAI Engineer

Integrates LLMs and AI APIs into products; builds RAG systems, AI agents, and LLM-powered features

Engineering Track
What This Role Actually Does

The "solid software engineer who works with AI" role. Rarely trains models from scratch. Integrates OpenAI/Anthropic/Gemini APIs, builds RAG pipelines, designs multi-step AI agents, implements prompt management systems, and ships AI-native product features.

E1 vs. E2 (ML Engineer)

Applied AI/GenAI Engineer: Integrates existing models into products. ML Engineer: Trains, optimizes, and serves models. "Build a chatbot" → E1. "Optimize inference latency for a 70B model" → E2.

Skills
Strong Python / SWE fundamentalsLLM API integrationRAG architecture (vector DBs, chunking, retrieval)Prompt engineering & structured outputsLangChain / LlamaIndex / or equivalentTypeScript for full-stack AI featuresTraining models from scratch NOT requiredDeep ML theory NOT required
Green Signals
  • Has shipped an LLM-powered feature to real users
  • Understands retrieval eval — recall, precision, reranking
  • Has dealt with production LLM issues: hallucination, latency, cost
Red Signals
  • Portfolio is only tutorial reproductions from YouTube guides
  • Can't explain how RAG works at a component level
  • No production software engineering experience
Sourcing Channels
Highest
GitHub — contributors to LangChain, LlamaIndex, Haystack, DSPy
Highest
AI-native startup alumni — Adept, Character.ai, Inflection, Cohere, Together AI
Medium
Latent Space podcast / AI Engineer community

Owns the training, optimization, and serving infrastructure for ML models in a product context

Engineering Track

Sits between the Applied ML Scientist (who designs the model) and MLOps (who maintains the infra). ML Engineers write training loops, implement model architectures, optimize for throughput and latency, and build the serving layer.

AI Infra / Compiler Engineer

Hardware-level ML optimization: custom kernels, compiler backends, and chip-level performance tuning

Infrastructure Track
What This Role Actually Does

Operates at the lowest level of the ML stack — between ML frameworks and hardware. Writes CUDA kernels (CUTLASS, Triton), works on compiler optimization (TVM, XLA, torch.compile), and squeezes performance from accelerators (A100, H100, TPU). Extremely rare, extremely high-comp.

Skills

Red Signals

A1

Data Scientist

Derives insights and supports decisions through analysis, experimentation, and predictive modeling

Analytics Track
What This Role Actually Does

Uses statistical analysis, A/B testing, and ML (often classical) to answer business questions and support product decisions. Primary output is insight and recommendation, not a production model. Works closely with product and strategy teams.

Data Scientist vs. Applied ML Scientist

Data Scientist: produces insight, supports decisions. Applied ML Scientist: produces production ML systems running autonomously at scale. "Work with product teams to understand behavior" → A1. "Own the ranking model" → S1.

NLP / Large Language Models

Discipline · Research, Applied Science, and Engineering tracks

What It Is

Processing, understanding, and generating human language. Modern NLP is dominated by transformer-based LLMs. Sub-areas: tokenization, pretraining, instruction tuning, RLHF, RAG, information extraction, summarization, translation.

Key Companies

OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI, Cohere, Mistral, AI21, Hugging Face, Microsoft (Azure AI)

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Treating all NLP candidates as interchangeable. A fine-tuning specialist (S1) and a CUDA-level inference engineer (E2/I3) are completely different talent pools with no overlap.

👁

Computer Vision

Discipline · Engineering and Research tracks; strong C++ presence

What It Is

Extracting meaning from visual data. Core tasks: image classification, object detection, semantic/instance segmentation, pose estimation, optical flow, 3D reconstruction. Modern CV uses ViT, SAM, DINO, and diffusion models.

Key Domains

AV (Waymo, Tesla, Aurora), robotics, healthcare (PathAI, Recursion), security (Verkada), AR/VR (Apple Vision, Meta), content moderation

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Searching "Computer Vision" and getting NLP candidates who added it as a skill. Filter specifically: require CVPR/ICCV publications, C++ experience, or domain-specific tools (OpenCV, YOLO, Detectron2, SAM).

🎮

Reinforcement Learning & RLHF

Discipline · Research and Applied Science tracks; niche but high-value

What It Is

RL: training agents via reward signals. Classical RL (PPO, DQN, SAC) used in robotics and gaming. RLHF: the technique behind ChatGPT/Claude — training LLMs to follow instructions using human preference data. Also: RLAIF, DPO as a simpler alternative.

Key Companies

OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, Google Brain, Cohere, Tesla, Boston Dynamics, Figure AI, 1X Technologies

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Conflating RL and RLHF as one pool. Classical RL researchers (robotics, games) and RLHF practitioners (LLM alignment) use different tooling and rarely overlap.

📡

Recommendations & Ranking Systems

Discipline · Applied Science track; concentrated at consumer tech

What It Is

The retrieval-and-ranking pipeline powering social feeds, search, e-commerce, and content discovery. Key concepts: candidate generation, two-tower models, learning-to-rank, feature stores for real-time features, and multi-objective ranking.

Key Companies

Meta (Feed, Reels), TikTok/ByteDance, Netflix, Spotify, YouTube, Pinterest, LinkedIn, Amazon, Etsy, DoorDash

Sourcing Note

One of the most domain-locked ML disciplines. Recs/Ranking specialists rarely have strong NLP or CV skills — and vice versa. The RecSys conference is the primary academic venue.

🔀

Multimodal AI

Discipline · Research and Engineering tracks; fast-growing

What It Is

Models processing and generating across multiple modalities: text + image (GPT-4V, Claude 3, LLaVA), text + audio (Whisper), text + video (Sora, Gemini). Key architectures: CLIP, Flamingo, LLaVA, CogVLM.

Key Companies

OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Anthropic, Meta AI, Microsoft, Runway ML, HeyGen, ElevenLabs, Pika Labs, Stability AI

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Hiring a pure NLP researcher for a multimodal role. Multimodal requires fluency in both language and vision modeling. Screen for CLIP-style pretraining experience, image encoder knowledge (ViT, ConvNext), and cross-attention mechanisms.

🎨

Generative AI / Diffusion Models

Discipline · Research and Engineering tracks

What It Is

Models generating novel content: images, video, audio, 3D, code. Two main families: diffusion models (DDPM, Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, Sora) and autoregressive generation (GPT-4, LLaMA). Diffusion specialists need UNet/DiT architecture knowledge, noise schedules, classifier-free guidance.

Key Companies

Stability AI, Midjourney, Runway ML, Pika, Adobe (Firefly), NVIDIA (Edify), Black Forest Labs, Ideogram, ElevenLabs

Sourcing Note

Diffusion model researchers are concentrated in GitHub communities: Hugging Face Diffusers contributors, A1111/ComfyUI extension developers, and arxiv/ICLR/CVPR paper authors on generative topics.

🎙

Speech & Audio AI

Discipline · Research and Engineering tracks; specialized talent pool

What It Is

ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition), TTS (Text-to-Speech), speaker diarization, voice cloning, audio generation. Key tools: Whisper (ASR), XTTS, VALL-E (TTS), EnCodec. Speech engineers need signal processing fundamentals (FFT, spectrograms, mel-filterbanks).

Key Companies

ElevenLabs, Deepgram, AssemblyAI, OpenAI, Microsoft (Azure Speech), Amazon (Alexa), Apple (Siri), Google (WaveNet), Otter.ai

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Treating speech engineers as NLP engineers with audio experience. Speech processing is a distinct discipline with separate venues (Interspeech, ICASSP), different math (signal processing), and different models (CTC, codec models).

🛡

AI Safety & Alignment

Discipline · Research track; see also Role R3

What It Is

Ensuring AI systems behave safely. Sub-disciplines: interpretability (what do neurons/circuits represent), alignment (RLHF, Constitutional AI, DPO), robustness (adversarial inputs), red-teaming (systematic elicitation of harmful behaviors), and formal verification.

Key Organizations

Anthropic, OpenAI Safety team, Google DeepMind Safety, ARC Evals, Redwood Research, CHAI (Berkeley), UK AISI

Sourcing Note

This community is small, mission-driven, and tight-knit. Cold LinkedIn outreach has low yield. Community sourcing — Alignment Forum, MATS program — has much higher yield.

📱

Edge AI / On-device ML

Discipline · Engineering and Infrastructure tracks; hardware-adjacent

What It Is

Running ML inference on mobile, embedded, or IoT devices without cloud connectivity. Requires aggressive model compression: quantization (INT8/INT4), pruning, knowledge distillation, neural architecture search for mobile. Key runtimes: CoreML (Apple), TFLite (Android), ONNX Runtime, MediaPipe.

Key Companies

Apple (CoreML team), Google (TFLite), Qualcomm (AI Engine), ARM (Ethos NPU), Meta (on-device), Samsung, NVIDIA Jetson, Hailo, Syntiant

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Confusing Edge AI engineers with cloud ML engineers. Edge specialists think about memory budget in megabytes, latency in milliseconds on a 4-core CPU. Screen for: INT8/INT4 quantization experience, mobile benchmark results, CoreML/TFLite deployment.

🗃

SQL-to-Text / NL2SQL

Discipline · Applied Science and Engineering tracks

What It Is

Converting natural language questions into executable SQL queries. A specialized NLP application requiring both language model knowledge and deep SQL/database understanding. Key challenges: schema linking, handling ambiguity, multi-table JOINs. Benchmarks: Spider, BIRD, WikiSQL.

Key Companies

Databricks, Snowflake (Cortex), ThoughtSpot, Sigma Computing, Mode Analytics, Atlan, Seek AI, Defog.ai, Numbers Station

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Hiring an NLP engineer without SQL depth, or a SQL expert without NLP knowledge. NL2SQL requires both. Screen for: familiarity with Spider/BIRD benchmarks and schema-linking approaches.

📐

Classical ML & Algorithms

Discipline · Applied Science and Analytics tracks; foundational

What It Is

Pre-deep-learning ML methods dominating many production applications: gradient boosting (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost) for tabular data, logistic regression for interpretable models, SVMs, clustering, dimensionality reduction (PCA, UMAP). Heavily used in fraud, pricing, credit risk, supply chain, and search.

Key Companies

Stripe (fraud), Affirm (credit), Square, banks (JPM, Goldman AI), insurance (Lemonade), e-commerce pricing (Amazon, Wayfair), logistics (FedEx, UPS)

Sourcing Note

Don't overlook classical ML talent for fintech, insurance, and supply chain roles. XGBoost + feature engineering expertise often outperforms deep learning for tabular prediction. Kaggle is the highest-yield sourcing channel.

📈

Time Series & Forecasting

Discipline · Applied Science and Analytics tracks; specialized

What It Is

Predicting future values from sequential data. Methods range from classical (ARIMA, Holt-Winters, Prophet) to deep learning (LSTM, Temporal Fusion Transformer, N-BEATS, PatchTST). Domains: demand forecasting, financial time series, energy forecasting, anomaly detection in metrics.

Key Companies

Amazon (supply chain), Walmart Labs, UPS/FedEx, Uber (demand forecasting), Stripe (payment anomalies), Bloomberg/financial firms, Meta (infra capacity planning)

Most Common Sourcing Mistake

Hiring a general Data Scientist for a forecasting-heavy role without checking for time series-specific experience. Proper time series modeling requires understanding of seasonality, stationarity, autocorrelation, and correct cross-validation (no random splits).
